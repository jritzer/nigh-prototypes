# Nigh — Brand Color Rules (New Branding)

The rule of thumb: **blue leads, gradient supports.** Blue carries trust and action; the
multicolor gradient carries energy and vibe. Blue is the system; the gradient is the accent.

## The two systems

**Primary — Nigh Blue `#0E4EF5`**
Use for everything structural and actionable: the logo, primary buttons/CTAs, the nav CTA,
active/selected states, links, key stats, and focus rings. If a user is meant to *do*
something, it's blue.

**Accent — the gradient family** (purple `#6226E9`, teal `#41EFF9`, pink `#EC1977`)
Use for expression, never for the primary action: hero glows, section-divider bars,
`gradient-text` headline moments, card top-accents, hover flourishes, illustration and
"scene" moments. This is what keeps the site feeling high-energy and Gen-Z — just layered
*on top of* a blue foundation rather than carrying the structure.

## Logo

- **Primary logo = one-color.** White on dark surfaces (`Nigh_Logo_White.svg`), blue on
  light surfaces. Used in nav, footer, and anywhere the brand must be reliable.
- **Favicon = one-color mark** (`favicon.svg`) — blue pin, white N.
- **Multicolor logo (`Nigh_Logo.svg`) = accent only.** Reserve it for rare expressive
  moments (a launch hero, campaign art). It is not the system logo.

## Buttons

- **Primary:** solid Nigh Blue, white text.
- **Secondary:** outline / ghost.
- **Accent:** reserve a gradient or single accent color for at most one special CTA per view.
- Avoid three competing button colors in the same view.

## Quick do / don't

- ✅ Blue primary CTA, gradient bar accent above a section, gradient headline once per page.
- ✅ White one-color logo in the nav on the dark background.
- ❌ Pink or teal as the primary CTA color.
- ❌ Multicolor logo as the default/system mark.
- ❌ Gradient on the main action button.

## Accessibility

On the dark background (`#0B0B1A`), Nigh Blue is strong for buttons (white text on blue).
For small blue *text/links* on dark, use a slightly brightened blue if contrast looks thin.
